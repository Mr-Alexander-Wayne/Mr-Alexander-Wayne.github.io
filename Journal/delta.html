Day One

Still on the subject of sayings and expressions, they say don't judge a book by it's cover. Well, I'd be damned if I pick up a book that has 'Advanced Calculus' legibly written across the cover and hope to find 'Adventures of Winnie the Pooh' in it.

I understand that what these expression mean are sound sayings, but the choice of idioms is something to be revised

Even stuff like two wrongs don't make a right. Although I agree with this, mathematically, two wrongs do make a right

In any case, three lefts make a right

Day Six

After, "window shopping" on Apple's website, I realized that I am not sure which one I will prefer between a Macbook and a Macbook Air. I am not here to review products, but I used to want an Air 13", now I am not so sure.

Progress on the website has been nice. In just two days, I have been able to complete the basic work. I should have hooked it up to this website to show her a demo. I will leave a link when I do, but I would take it off again by Friday. So, if you want to view it, check day two of my next log.

Let me just say a few things about my weird self. I love puns and I love sarcasm. If you have no sense of humour, we would have a hard time relating. I generally like being alone and I am generally quiet (until you know me), but when I am in a new environment, I spend quite sometime studying people. People say I ask too much questions. That is a part of me I have come to terms with.

I like movies, food and making things. I wish I could draw, but I express creativity through other mediums; playing keyboard, writing, designing things or cooking. I think I am better than the "Average Joe", however I am far from content with my life. I read somewhere that you should always try to improve yourself a little each day. That is what I am trying to do

Speaking of which, I like to read - amazingly, I hate reading school books. I like to think all writers are readers and vice versa.
